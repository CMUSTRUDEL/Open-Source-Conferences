Publication date: 2020-09-18

Description: 
	Would Open Source Trademarks Benefit From a Foundation?
Pamela Chestek 

In July, Google announced the creation of the Open Usage Commons, described as a neutral, independent ownership for trademarks to “give peace of mind regarding their use of project names in a fair and transparent way.” Unique challenges specific to licensing of trademarks aside, are there problems with how the open source world uses trademarks? If so, is an independent entity the best way to solve the problems?
